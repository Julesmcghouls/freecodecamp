Poké Balls

Poké Balls are essential tools for capturing Pokémon. There are different types of Poké Balls, each with different effectiveness.

Poké Ball Great Ball Ultra Ball

Basic Poké Balls have a 50% catch rate under normal conditions, while Great Balls have a 75% catch rate.

  • Poké Ball: Standard ball used in catching Pokémon.
  • Great Ball: Offers a higher catch rate than a Poké Ball.
  • Ultra Ball: One of the most reliable Poké Balls.
Finding Pokémon

Pokémon can be found in various locations such as grassy areas, caves, or bodies of water. You can also find them by using certain items.

Fishing Rod Repel Pokémon Radar

Some Pokémon can only be found by using specific items like the Fishing Rod in aquatic areas.

  • Grassy areas: Common places to find wild Pokémon.
  • Caves: Home to rare Pokémon species.
  • Water: Fishing rods are used to catch water-type Pokémon.
Master Ball

The Master Ball is a special Poké Ball with a 100% catch rate. It is typically only available once in each game.

Master Ball

Use the Master Ball carefully as it is a rare item, usually given after a special event in the game.

  • Guaranteed success: 100% catch rate on any Pokémon.
  • Best used for Legendary Pokémon or difficult-to-catch Pokémon.
Trading Pokémon

Trading Pokémon is a special method of catching Pokémon where players exchange Pokémon with others.

Link Cable Global Trade System

Some Pokémon can only be obtained by trading with friends or other players across regions.

  • Link Cable: A method of trading in earlier games.
  • Global Trade System: A modern way to trade Pokémon online.
Special Catching Methods

Some Pokémon require special conditions to be caught, such as certain times of day or unique in-game events.

Safari Zone Night Catching Special Events

For example, some Pokémon can only be caught during specific in-game events or certain times of day.

  • Safari Zone: A special area where you can catch rare Pokémon.
  • Night Catching: Certain Pokémon are only active at night.
  • Special Events: Periodic events where special Pokémon are made available for a limited time.
